🪐 Planet Descriptions

☀️ Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system. It provides the light and heat necessary for life on Earth and governs the orbits of all planets.

🧿 Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in the solar system. It has a rocky surface, extreme temperature swings, and no atmosphere to speak of.

🟡 Venus

Venus is similar in size to Earth but has a thick, toxic atmosphere and surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead. It rotates in the opposite direction to most planets.

🌍 Earth

Earth is our home planet and the only known world to support life. It has a rich diversity of ecosystems, liquid water, and a protective atmosphere.

🔴 Mars

Mars is known as the Red Planet due to its iron oxide-rich soil. It has the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system and is a prime candidate for future exploration.

🟠 Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, a gas giant with a powerful magnetic field and dozens of moons. Its Great Red Spot is a giant storm larger than Earth.

🪐 Saturn

Saturn is famous for its stunning ring system, made of ice and rock. It is a gas giant with over 80 moons, including the intriguing Titan.

🔵 Uranus

Uranus is an ice giant with a blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere. It rotates on its side, making its seasons extreme and unique.

🌀 Neptune

Neptune is the farthest known planet from the Sun. It is a deep blue ice giant with supersonic winds and a faint ring system.

❄️ Pluto

Pluto, once considered the ninth planet, is now classified as a dwarf planet. It has a heart-shaped glacier and a thin atmosphere that expands as it approaches the Sun.
